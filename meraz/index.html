Shredded(Battle of Bands)

  • Description

    Does your band possess the ability to fill the audience with energy and wilderness by propulsive backbeats and passionately driven guitars featuring a charismatic voice? “Shredded” presented by Meraz is just the right arena for your band.

  • Rules
    • All Artists must perform and compete on behalf of only one band.
    • Bands must submit a one song demo in mp4 format (to ashitabhm@iitbhilai.ac.in via mail or google drive < Subject: Shredded Meraz’18 >) (contact no. has to be provided via mail). Top 5 Bands will be performing in the fest. The deadline for video submission is 30th September 2018.
    • There is no limit to the number of bands who wish to apply for the competition and all submitted demos will be fairly appraised for shortlisting.
    • Band must not consist of more than 7 members.
    • Your set should contain no vulgarities, profanities, lewd comments, racist remarks, violence,sexual content, etc.
    • You must provide your own instruments, amplifiers, and cables. (Drums will be provided by us)
      Note: The Event is on the main stage outdoor and we will have the necessary setup in regards to speakers but participants are advised to get their own cables to avoid any last minute inconvenience.
    • The winner will be chosen from a three judge panel; two songs will be performed, judges will critique the performance and provide feedback with recommendations. Audience participation will factor into the decision.
    • Performers will have 20 minutes on stage, which includes setup, performance time, and take down.
    • No backing Tracks allowed

  • Judging Criteria

    • Planning/Preparedness

      Material: How unique did the songs sound? Even if it is a cover song, did they spin their own creativity into it?

      Appearance: Did the band look original? Did the band stand out?

      Improvisation: Did the band demonstrate any level of original improvisational skill? I.e. ‘jam out’ or vocal through an instrument change, repair or equipment failure.

    • Interface with Audience

      Verbal Interaction: Did the band talk to or with the audience?

      Physical Interaction: Did the band physically participate with the audience e.g. Eye contact, head nod, acknowledgments, etc.

      Confidence: Was the band confident in their interaction with the audience?

    • Quality of Performance

      Confidence/Competence: Did the band look at ease whilst playing? Did the band appear to be able to do what they wanted to do?

      Set Dynamism: Did the band’s setlist appear to play according to a plan i.e. was it well constructed and able to manipulate the audience’s mood?

      Stage Presence: Did the band use the stage effectively? Did the band appear positive about their performance? Did the band interact effectively on stage? How did the band cope with any problems they may have encountered?

      Conformance to contest rules: Did the band followed the rules stated in the contest i.e. time limit, profanity?

Arena Haste

Arena Haste

    “Obstacles are put in your way to see if what you want is really worth fighting for”. Make a bot that can run for you and cross the final line before anyone else does.

  • Description
    Round 1

    It is a beauty round and round 2 is arena rush.

    Round 2
    Every team will have to build a bot following the restrictions that will be provided. The team’s driver may control the bot and drive over the arena. Arena may or may not contain moving obstacles. The team that completes the arena in minimum time will be the winners. The arena contains objects that need to be lifted and there are also some timing based hurdles

  • Rules
      Round 1
    • Winning team will be decided based on voting process.
    • The voting will be based only on the aesthetics of the bot. Voters will neither be given any information about the team or the features of the bot.
    • Votes to the bot from the same team will not be considered.

      • Round 2
    • The whole round is time-based.
    • Only one bot will be allowed in the arena at a time.
    • There can be, at max, five-member in each team.
    • Rush starts when the Coordinator announces the same and will end when the bot crosses the finish Line or accomplish a certain number of penalties (the number will be told at the event day).
    • Ranks would be allotted to all the teams based on the points would be given accordingly to them.
    • The bots must not cross the path specified OR must not take shortcuts.
    • Only 1 person – is allowed to control the bot. Rest of the team members are NOT allowed to enter the boundary
    • In case of BOT DAMAGE / Battery Drain in between the competition, a time penalty will be given. Maximum of 2 team members (excluding controller) are allowed to hop in the track and immediately repair it (this time shall not be counted). The bot will have to continue from the last checkpoint where it stopped.
    • No other people (except Coordinator, Camera Man, & the Managing Committee) are allowed to enter the race track until the last bot finishes the race.
    • Any team who’s bot is violating the specifications of bots given below, shall be disqualified.
    • Incase of any damage caused by the bot to the arena, the team (with the help of the organisers) must repair the arena. However in case of irreparable damages, the team shall be disqualified and the event will be continued with changes in the arena (with mutual agreement between the teams)
    • Details about any new penalties or rules that is not discussed in this document will be announced at the start of the event.
    • In case of any dispute, the decision taken by the management committee will be final.

      • NOTE: The above rules are subject to change. In such genuine cases, the modified rules would be informed by the Coordinator.

      Specifications of the bot:
    • The maximum cost of the bot must be less than 6000₹.
    • Bot must be having a pickup mechanism to lift a block (perfect cube) of side length 15cm and weighing upto 0.5kg.
    • The maximum and minimum dimensions (LxWxH) of the bot can be 30cm x 30cm x 30cm (excluding the pickup mechanism) and 10cm x 10cm x 5cm.
    • No bot can weigh more than 2kg (along with the powersource).
    • The choice of control mechanism (i.e. wired or wireless) is left to the team.

      • **All the teams must​ register at least 1 week before the event.
      **All the participating teams are requested to keep checking this section of the Meraz’s website for more updates.
